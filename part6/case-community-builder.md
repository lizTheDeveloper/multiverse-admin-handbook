**Freire's Problem-Posing Education:**

**Relational Accountability:**

## Weeks 7-9: Logistics & Safety

> - Protect vulnerable members from harassment
> - Address ableist language/behavior specifically

## ADHD Hyperfocus vs. Mania: How to Tell

**This case study also illustrates the difference:**

### ADHD Hyperfocus (Sam)
- Intense interest and energy around one topic
- Can pause and reflect when prompted
- Accepts feedback and adjusts
- Sleeps normally, eats normally, maintains other responsibilities
- Acknowledges their pattern of starting/abandoning projects
- Vision is ambitious but grounded in reality
- Follows through on structured process

### Mania (See "Aspiring Guru" case study)
- Grandiose beliefs ("I'm chosen to transform education")
- Can't pause or reflectâ€”driven quality
- Rejects feedback, gets defensive
- Not sleeping, reckless decisions
- Denies or doesn't see pattern
- Vision involves magical thinking
- Bypasses structure, recruits immediately

**Key difference:** Sam had enthusiasm + groundedness. Manic episodes lack groundedness.

## Lessons for Students with Visions

### If you're a student with a big idea:

- You can articulate your "why" beyond "it sounds cool"

- You need it to be BIG and SPECIAL
- You bypass structure because "you're different"
- You get defensive when limitations are named

**The 90-Day Track isn't punishment. It's preparation.**
